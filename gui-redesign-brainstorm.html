Design critique · hn-slow-rank · 2026-06-09

The magic moment is set in 10px gray type

The shipped page is an honest, competent tool — and it reads like a script's output, because it is one. Mass appeal needs the product's one astonishing fact (“the best comment was buried at #246”) to be the interface, not a footnote pill on it.

The ask
Analyze the GUI. Brainstorm the best redesign that maximizes the chance of mass appeal of our website.

Diagnosis in three sentences

1. The page borrows HN's beige skin, so to outsiders it looks like the thing it's fixing — and to insiders it looks like a userscript, not a product. It has no visual identity of its own.

2. The hierarchy is inverted: the rescue story (“was #246 by votes → surfaced”) is the entire thesis and the most shareable fact on the page, yet it's the smallest element, while undifferentiated story titles get the largest type.

3. There is no share loop at all — zero Open Graph / Twitter meta tags, no per-story anchors, no quotable unit. A pasted link renders as a bare URL. For mass distribution this is the single cheapest, highest-leverage fix in the whole brainstorm.

1

No identity, no hero. A 20px system-font title over a borrowed beige. Nothing communicates “someone made this and it's alive” — no logo mark, no freshness signal above the fold, no reason to remember it.

2

Legend-and-stats soup before any content. Seven emoji chips and five mono stats demand decoding before the reader sees a single comment. Mass-audience readers don't study legends; the meaning must ride on the content itself.

3

The thesis is the smallest thing on screen. “was #1231 by votes → surfaced” is a 6.5px gray pill. That line is the product. It should be the headline device, not metadata.

4

~150 reading decisions, zero editing. 28 stories × up to 6 comments, every card identical weight, no featured moment, no “if you read one thing today.” Mass appeal requires an editor's cut, not a mirror of the firehose.

5

Dead-end page. No share affordance, no OG cards, no “how scoring works” link, no way to subscribe to tomorrow. Every visit is a cul-de-sac; growth has no mechanism.

What's genuinely working — keep all of it: the microcopy voice (“hide the fight”, “Expand if you want the fight”), the legible why-floated reasons under each comment, and the honest accounting that separates “hidden as low-signal” from “ordinary, just not top-ranked.” The redesign should amplify this voice, not replace it.

Four directions

Distinctly different bets, each with the tradeoff it's making named first. Mini-mockups are directional, not pixel-final.

Optimizes for shareability + daily ritual

A · The Daily Rescue

Stop mirroring the front page; become an editorial product. The hero is one rescued comment, set like a magazine pull quote, with its rescue story told big: “buried at #1231 by votes.” Below it, “today's rescues” — the 10 best across all threads — then the full per-story digest for completists. Warm-cream editorial design, serif display, one accent. Every rescue gets a permalink and a screenshot-ready quote card.

B · The X-ray (before / after)

Make the re-ranking itself the interface: two columns per thread — “by votes” vs “by substance” — with the rescued comment visibly teleporting up and the duels (striped) sinking. Nobody needs the legend explained; the picture is the explanation. Strongest possible launch/demo page and the natural Show HN screenshot.

C · The Calm Reader

Compete with read-it-later apps, not with HN. One thread at a time, big serif body type, generous margins, the surfaced comments presented as a readable digest essay with author chips — swipe / arrow-key between stories, dark mode, installable PWA. The anti-doomscroll positioning (“read 10 good comments, then stop”) is itself a mass-market hook.

D · HN-native polish

Keep the beige deliberately — but commit to it as a costume, worn better: an HN-orange masthead bar (instantly legible as “an HN thing”), max one badge + one plain-English reason per comment, top-3 not top-6, collapse stats into a single sentence. The page reads as “HN, but the good comments” instead of “a script's report.”

Recommendation

Build A with B as its hero device. The Daily Rescue is the only direction that scores high on all three mass-appeal mechanics at once — instant comprehension, a quotable unit, and a daily ritual — and it's cheap because the generator already computes everything it needs (scores, reasons, dfsIndex). Embed one compact X-ray strip (B) at the top of the page as the permanent 3-second explainer of how the rescue happened; keep the full per-story digest below for power readers, in the current honest voice. Direction C is the v2 if retention data says people come back; D's “one badge + plain-English reason” compression should be adopted inside A regardless.

Concretely: hero rescue with serif pull quote + “rescued from #N” as the brand device → X-ray strip → “Today's 10 rescues” list with permalinks → per-story digest. New name surface: keep Slow Rank, drop “Hot Thread” from user-facing copy.

Ship regardless of direction (this week)

unblock

OG / Twitter meta tags + favicon. The page currently has none — every shared link renders bare. Generate a per-day OG image (the hero rescue as a quote card) in the same CI run. Highest leverage-to-effort ratio on this entire page.

unblock

Per-story anchors + a share link on every rescue. The unit of virality is the rescued comment, not the homepage. #story-48463808 anchors exist for free; add a “copy link” affordance.

cheap

Collapse the header. One sentence (“3,628 comments read so you don't have to — updated 14 min ago”) replaces the stats row + legend. The freshness chip doubles as a liveness signal.

cheap

One badge max + plain-English reason. “🔗⟨/⟩✋📊” becomes “brought receipts: code + benchmarks.” The reasons array already contains this; it's a rendering change.

cheap

Trust link in the footer of every story card: “how scoring works” → README anchor, plus the calibration line (“weights validated against blind pairwise judgments, 81% held-out agreement”). Mass audiences don't read methods; they notice that methods exist.
